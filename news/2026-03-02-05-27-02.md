#### Anthropic — Government Ban: Pentagon Standoff Escalates to Federal Phase-Out

**Source:** [Anthropic Rejects Pentagon — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/26/tech/anthropic-rejects-pentagon-offer) | [Trump Orders Phase-Out — Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/trump-us-government-anthropic-claude-pentagon-6-months-phaseout-ai-standoff/) | [Pentagon Supply-Chain Risk — Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/02/27/trump-anthropic-claude-drop/) | [Federal Agencies at Risk — Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/27/claude-anthropic-trump-government-agencies) | [Blacklist — CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/27/trump-anthropic-ai-pentagon.html)

**What Changed:**
- **Pentagon Demand (Feb 26)**: Defense Secretary Hegseth demanded Anthropic allow Claude for "all lawful purposes" without restrictions on autonomous weapons or mass domestic surveillance. $200M contract at stake.
- **Anthropic Rejection (Feb 26)**: CEO Dario Amodei responded: "We cannot in good conscience accede to their request." Anthropic refused to lift safeguards.
- **Government Response (Feb 27)**: Trump ordered all federal agencies to "immediately cease" using Anthropic technology. Hegseth designated Anthropic a "Supply-Chain Risk to National Security."
- **Phase-Out**: 6-month phase-out for agencies including Pentagon, HHS, OPM, DoE, NASA JPL.
- **Cascade Effects**: OneGov deal terminated. Pulled from USAi.gov and Multiple Award Schedule. Defense contractors must certify no Claude usage.
- **Enterprise Ripple**: Any company doing business with the U.S. military must now prove they don't use Anthropic products in their workflows.

**Impact on You:**
- *Vendor risk assessment required*: If your healthcare company serves VA hospitals, military health systems (TRICARE), or any government-adjacent entity, the supply-chain risk designation directly affects your ability to use Claude in those workflows. Conduct an immediate audit of government touchpoints.
- *Private sector unaffected — for now*: The ban targets federal agencies and contractors, not private healthcare. However, enterprise customers who also serve government may face pressure. Monitor for expansion beyond federal scope.
- *Anthropic's long-term viability*: Anthropic loses government revenue and credibility as a "safe" enterprise vendor. The safety stance that makes Anthropic attractive for healthcare (controlled, safety-first AI) is the exact stance that triggered the ban. This creates a paradox: the safety features you want are the features the government won't accept.

**Disruption Score: 10/10** — Conduct immediate vendor risk assessment. Audit all government touchpoints. Develop contingency plan for multi-vendor AI strategy. Do NOT abandon Anthropic for private-sector work — the safety stance is a feature, not a bug — but document your exposure.
